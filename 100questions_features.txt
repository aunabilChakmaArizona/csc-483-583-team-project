What That Means For Retrieval

Named entities matter most. Many clues contain strong proper-noun phrases like Naples Museum of Art, James B. Stewart, Bernt Balchen, Pierre Cauchon, UCLA.
Temporal clues are common enough to exploit. Years show up in 41% of the set, so keeping years in the query is useful.
Quoted spans are valuable. They often point to exact lyrics, titles, or famous quotations, which can strongly match article lead text or body.
Category can help on ambiguous clues. For example NEWSPAPERS, BROADWAY LYRICS, AFRICAN CITIES, GOLDEN GLOBE WINNERS can disambiguate otherwise vague text.
Lead text is likely useful. A lot of clues describe an entity via one defining fact, which often appears in the first sentence or first paragraph.
